Case Study: "Own Goal" & "Nautilus's Conundrum"

Problem: Creating engaging and introspective games that communicate meaningful messages.
Audience: Gamers interested in unique and thoughtful game experiences.
Project Name: "Own Goal" & "Nautilus's Conundrum"
Type of Project: Game Design and Development
Duration: 3 months
Team and Role: Individual project, responsible for all aspects of design and development.
Process: Iterative design and development, starting with concept creation, followed by prototyping, testing, and refinement.
Methods: GameSalad for development, playtesting for feedback, and iterative improvements.
Deliverables: Functional game prototypes, project documentation, and a case study report.
These were my second and third projects for my Designing Playful Experiences course at UM, which were designed and coded in GameSalad. These functional prototypes are among some of my first forays into game design and development.
Own Goal: A game where the player controls a 'goal' and is tasked with making sure the ball does not go past them.
Nautilus's Conundrum: A game where the player is a canine astronaut traversing an interstellar maze using only their head and a webcam.
